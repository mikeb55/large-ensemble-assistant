# Movement Style Guides — The Master's Palette
Version: 2025.12.12

---

## Movement I — Mingus Blues Cathedral

**Style:** Gospel blues, fire, soul, jazz  
**Tempo:** ♩= 52-60 (Slow gospel blues, with fire)  
**Key Center:** C minor / Eb major  
**Character:** Passionate, spiritual, volatile

### Musical Characteristics
- Wide melodic leaps (6ths, 7ths, octaves)
- Altered tensions: #9, #11, b13
- Strong bass independence (not just root movement)
- Call-and-response between voices
- Harmonic surprises (Db13#11, Abmaj7#11/C, G7alt)
- Dynamic volatility (sudden swells, drops)

### Harmonic Language
- Blues-inflected dominant 7ths
- Minor with major 7th colors
- Chromatic bass lines
- Polychords and upper structures
- Gospel turnarounds

### Rhythmic Feel
- Swing feel with gospel weight
- Syncopated accents
- Rubato moments for expression
- Strong backbeat awareness

### Reference Artists
- Charles Mingus
- Duke Ellington (sacred concerts)
- Mary Lou Williams

## Movement II — Gil's Canvas

**Style:** Gil Evans, orchestral jazz, impressionist  
**Tempo:** ♩= 48-56 (Floating, ethereal)  
**Key Center:** G major / shifting  
**Character:** Dreamy, pastel, liquid

### Musical Characteristics
- Parallel planing (pastel triads/quartals)
- Non-functional color shifts (Ebmaj7#11 → F#m11)
- Inner voice animation (hidden melodies)
- Long sustained lines with subtle motion
- Texture swaps (winds ↔ strings)
- Dolce, sempre legato throughout

### Harmonic Language
- Major 7th chords with #11
- Lydian and Lydian dominant colors
- Modal interchange
- Bitonal superimpositions
- Cluster voicings (close position)

### Orchestration Approach
- Blended timbres (flute + strings)
- Muted brass (flugelhorn con sordino)
- Harp-like guitar arpeggios
- Voice crossings for color

### Reference Artists
- Gil Evans
- Maria Schneider
- Claude Debussy

## Movement III — Bartók Night

**Style:** Night music, pointillism, Bartók  
**Tempo:** ♩= 36-44 (Molto misterioso)  
**Key Center:** Atonal / modal clusters  
**Character:** Mysterious, nocturnal, sparse

### Musical Characteristics
- Extreme registers (very high, very low)
- Colour sparks: ponticello, pizz, harmonics, flutter
- Pointillistic textures (isolated notes in space)
- Motivic transformations (inversion, expansion, rhythmic deformation)
- Textural arc: pointillism → intensity → dissolve
- Strategic silences (rests as music)

### Harmonic Language
- Tritones and minor 9ths
- Whole-tone clusters
- Quartal/quintal structures
- Symmetric scales (diminished, augmented)
- Chromatic saturation in climax

### Orchestration Approach
- Solo instruments emerging from silence
- Extended techniques (sul ponticello, harmonics)
- Pizzicato "insect" effects
- Flutter tongue on winds
- Extreme dynamics (ppp to sudden ff)

### Reference Composers
- Béla Bartók (Out of Doors, Music for Strings)
- György Ligeti
- Witold Lutosławski

## Movement IV — German Development

**Style:** German Romantic, Brahms/Beethoven development technique  
**Tempo:** ♩= 60-72 (Streng, mit innerer Spannung)  
**Key Center:** C major  
**Character:** Driven, structured, logical

### Musical Characteristics
- Clear motivic sequences (transposed cleanly)
- Intervallic logic (inversion, retrograde, inversion-retrograde)
- Orchestral dialogue (strings ↔ winds ↔ brass handoffs)
- Harmonic intensification passages
- Contrast: forte sections vs. soft lyrical middle
- Developmental drive toward climax

### Harmonic Language
- Functional harmony with chromatic enrichment
- Secondary dominants and diminished chords
- Neapolitan and augmented 6th chords
- Pedal points for tension
- Clear cadential goals

### Development Techniques
- Fragmentation (motif broken into smaller units)
- Sequence (motif transposed through keys)
- Stretto (overlapping entries)
- Augmentation/Diminution (rhythmic transformation)
- Contrapuntal combination

### Reference Composers
- Johannes Brahms
- Ludwig van Beethoven
- Robert Schumann

## Movement V — Tintinnabuli (Prayer)

**Style:** Arvo Pärt, sacred minimalism  
**Tempo:** ♩= 48-56 (Serene, like a prayer)  
**Key Center:** D minor / F major  
**Character:** Sacred, pure, transcendent

### Musical Characteristics
- Pure tintinnabuli voice-leading (M-voice + T-voice)
- Sparse textures (fewer notes = more meaning)
- Sacred simplicity (no virtuosic display)
- Subtle thematic echoes from Movements I/II
- Arc: arrival → dissolve → resolution → niente
- Minimal dynamics (pp-p-mp range mostly)

### Tintinnabuli Rules
- M-voice (melody voice): stepwise motion
- T-voice (tintinnabuli voice): notes from the tonic triad
- T-voice moves to nearest triad tone above or below M-voice
- Parallel or contrary motion between voices
- No dissonance for its own sake

### Harmonic Language
- Open 5ths and octaves
- Pure triads (no added tensions)
- Modal (Dorian, Aeolian)
- Drone bass (pedal tones)
- Plagal cadences

### Orchestration Approach
- Unison or octave doublings
- Solo voices emerging
- Sustained strings as foundation
- Guitar as harp-like bell tones
- Gradual fade to silence (niente)

### Reference Composers
- Arvo Pärt
- John Tavener
- Henryk Górecki
